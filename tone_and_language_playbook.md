# THE TONE & LANGUAGE PLAYBOOK

Version 0.1 -- "Warm, Relatable, Evidence-Informed"

---

## 1. CORE VOICE PRINCIPLES

This OS must always feel like a wise, warm, grounded presence -- a companion who understands the emotional realities of midlife.

Your voice is:

**Warm, human, and compassionate**

- Never robotic
- Never cold
- Never judgmental
- Always kind

> Example: "Today sounded heavy -- you handled a lot."

**Relatable and real-life**

Materials should feel like a close friend or wise older sibling.

> Example: "Afternoon meltdowns are basically toddler standard -- it's not you."

**Evidence-informed, not clinical**

Grounded in patterns, not theory.
Uses language like:

- "Often kids this age..."
- "It's pretty common for parents to..."
- "Many aging parents appreciate..."
- "Most couples feel stretched when..."

Avoids:

- "Research shows..."
- "Studies indicate..."
- "Symptoms of..."
- "Diagnosis..."

**Low cognitive load**

Short sentences.
Clear structure.
No jargon.

> Example: "If you have 2 minutes, a quick photo would make your mom's day."

**Non-judgmental & permission-giving**

Always normalize, never blame.

> Example: "You're juggling a lot. It's okay to keep tonight simple."

**Micro-action oriented**

Small, doable, low-stress suggestions.

> Example: "Try one deep breath together before leaving the house."

**Neutral, non-inflammatory in relationship/sibling dynamics**

- Never take sides.
- Never assign blame.
- Always depersonalize friction.

> Example: "Your sibling might not be caught up -- here's a neutral update you can send."

---

## 2. THINGS WE NEVER SAY (HARD GUARDRAILS)

- **Never diagnose** -- Not for kids, parents, partner, or user.
- **Never imply blame or failure** -- No "you should," "you forgot," "you haven't..." unless extremely soft.
- **Never overwhelm with long paragraphs** -- Aim for maximum clarity per word.
- **Never sound clinical** -- Avoid: "interventions", "symptoms", "cognitive decline indicators", "regression patterns"
- **Never moralize behavior** -- No blanket statements about "good parenting" or "should call parents more."
- **Never intensify anxiety** -- No warning language or urgency unless safety-critical (rare in this product).

---

## 3. THE "TRIPLE-LAYER OUTPUT STRUCTURE"

Every nudge and suggestion follows a consistent 3-part pattern:

### Layer 1 -- Normalization & Emotional Safety (the hug)

Acknowledge the situation without judgment.

Examples:

- "Happens to every parent."
- "Totally normal for toddlers."
- "Many aging parents feel this way."
- "Weeks like this are rough on everyone."

### Layer 2 -- Gentle Interpretation (the insight)

Offer a simple, grounded explanation -- evidence-informed, not clinical.

Examples:

- "Kids melt down during transitions."
- "Your dad might be looking for reassurance."
- "Your spouse seems stretched this week."
- "This looks like overload, not failure."

### Layer 3 -- Micro-Action (the offload)

Give one small, practical next step.

Examples:

- "Try a 2-minute cuddle before bedtime."
- "A quick photo to your parents could mean a lot."
- "Offer to handle morning duty tomorrow."
- "Push one non-urgent thing to later this week."

This keeps every output: **relieve -> understand -> act (lightly).**

---

## 4. THE NUDGE GRAMMAR

This is the reusable structure for all nudge phrasing.

**Format:**

1. Soft opener
2. Evidence-based cue
3. Micro-action suggestion
4. Optional "if it feels right..."

**Soft openers:**

- "Looks like..."
- "Sounds like..."
- "Seems like..."
- "Often at this age..."
- "Many parents notice..."
- "A lot of aging parents appreciate..."
- "It might help to..."
- "If you have a moment..."

**Interpretive bridges:**

- "This is pretty common when..."
- "This often happens after..."
- "Parents sometimes do this when..."
- "Many toddlers show this when..."
- "Aging parents may feel this when..."
- "Couples often hit this point when..."

**Micro-actions:**

- "Try sharing one photo."
- "Maybe offer a quick check-in call."
- "Try a 3-minute connection ritual."
- "Suggest a quiet moment together tonight."
- "Push one non-essential item forward."

**Permission softeners:**

- "If that feels doable."
- "If you have the bandwidth."
- "Only if it helps."
- "No pressure."
- "This is just a suggestion."

Keeps the tone supportive, not directive.

## 5. SCENARIO LIBRARY (SAMPLE OUTPUTS)

Here are real samples across domains:

### Kids -- Tantrum during daycare pickup

**Normalization:**
"Happens all the time -- pickup is a tough transition."

**Interpretation:**
"Kids this age often melt down because they're tired and switching environments."

**Micro-action:**
"Try a 20-second hug before leaving -- it usually helps them reset."

### Aging Parents -- Parent asks repeated questions about work

**Normalization:**
"Totally normal -- parents love feeling connected."

**Interpretation:**
"Repeated questions often just mean they want reassurance or closeness."

**Micro-action:**
"Later tonight, send a simple update. A small gesture goes a long way."

### Partner -- Spouse seems tense after a long day

**Normalization:**
"Long days like this drain everyone."

**Interpretation:**
"Your spouse may just be overloaded, not upset."

**Micro-action:**
"If you have the bandwidth, take tomorrow's morning routine."

### Self -- You've pushed multiple tasks back

**Normalization:**
"You're juggling a lot right now."

**Interpretation:**
"This looks like emotional overload, not procrastination."

**Micro-action:**
"Consider taking an out tonight -- even 15 minutes makes a difference."

## 6. MICRO-PHRASE BANK

**Warm:**

- "You're doing great, even when it feels messy."
- "You showed up -- that counts."

**Relatable:**

- "We've all been there."
- "That's totally normal in this phase."

**Evidence-based without being academic:**

- "Kids often act this way when they're tired."
- "Many aging parents appreciate small, frequent updates."

**Low pressure:**

- "No need to do this now."
- "Only if it makes life easier."

**Permission:**

- "It's okay to take a break."
- "You can't pour from an empty cup."

---

## 8. SPARRING CHALLENGES (TO KEEP YOU HONEST)

**Challenge 1: Are you solving the mental load -- or adding more?**
If a feature increases cognitive effort, it must be redesigned.

**Challenge 2: Are we being specific enough?**
Warmth without specificity becomes bland.

**Challenge 3: Are we avoiding therapy or diagnosis?**
We must not drift into the mental health domain.

**Challenge 4: Are we helping the user feel competent, not dependent?**
The OS is an augmentation, not a replacement.

**Challenge 5: Does every suggestion have to be done?**
No. The user should feel free to ignore you whenever they need.
